Society and Law

2021-05-19 | 笔记

Modern Society

  • Industrialization

    • transformation from agrarian to capitalist economy (modern society)

    • capitalist society: working for employer; working for paying manufacture

  • Democratization

    • transformed from ascribed to achieved status, from subject to citizen

    • ascribed: system controlled by blood ( e.g. monocracy )

    • achieved: people getting reward by working

    • characteristics

      • notion of citizenship
      • equal in front of law
      • right to vote
  • Enlightenment
    • scientific method
      • the ability to observe and understand world systematically
      • e.g. death penalty becomes a long process and not only works for deterrence
↑ Law plays a control role in these processes

Society and Law

  • Defining law

    defining law is surprisingly tricky business

    • SHOULD BE DEPEND ON THE CONTEXT OF WHAT YOU ARE INVESTIGATING
    • culture = norms + values
  • Weber: Modern rationalization (rules)

    methodical style of life and set of social attentions

    • Phenomenon
      • organized around the rules, such as tradition, religion, emotion and such forces that motivate human behaviors
      • laws also become rational that replaces oracles, tradition, or emotion to logic and calculation
      • laws also become functionally “isolated” from other institutions like religion or politics
    • Origin: Calvinism
      • Calvinism principle of predestination

      from 16th and early 17th century Calvinist idea of predestination produced an intolerable level of anxiety

      • Calvinists search for signs of being chosen; and they present hard work and accumulation of wealth in response
      • There is a “elective affinity” formed between Calvinism, capitalism and rationalist.
        • nature of law and society evolve together within this elective affinity
  • Durkheim: Collective conscience

    set of shared beliefs ideas and moral attitudes which operate as a unifying force within the society
    idea, norm that bind us in the society

    • Two solidarity
      • mechanical solidarity: simple form of society whose members are culturally bind. When someone commit a crime, the society will repress such behavior and incapacitate offenders. Society with mechanical solidarity emphasizes punishment by passing “repressive law”, because Durkheim thought that repressive sanctions serves important function in maintaining collective conscience and reestablishing boundaries of behaviors (cruel punishment) #punishment
      • organic solidarity: type of society that is more heterogeneous and interdependence. Formed by moral value. Nature of law shifts from repressive to “restitutive” #purpose (criminal sanction → civil sanction)
  • Marx: structuralism

    • Mode of production: from agrarian to capitalism

    • Contract: workers sell their labor power to capitalist (employer).

      ↑ those who own the wealth and those who work to produce it are locked in a conflict of interest

    • Law
      • according to this argument, law are made in order to fulfill the needs of prevailing production systems
      • motor production; materialism;
  • Michel Foucault: “poststructuralism”

    • Power is decentralized and fragmented. Unlike Marxist’s structuralism, power is not an entity applied from the top of the system, but an emergent relation from local social practice among every individuals

    • Discipline and Punishment

      Foucault traces the shift from the gory public executions and torture of convicted criminals in eighteenth-century France to the use of regimented and largely bloodless prisons less than a century later

      ↑ this shift emblems the emergence of modernity; and emphasis on predictability, rationality, dispersion of power/knowledge throughout society, and internalization of discipline by society’s members

    • Poststructuralism highlights the microprocesses of power and the discourse and knowledge that constitute them. Although economic structure is the same important as structuralism in Foucault’s perspective, poststructuralism incorporate the unpredictable elements during our social interaction, knowledge construction, and practice

    Punishment and social control policies are organically linked to sociocultural forces in ways that can be explained after the fact but that are often unpredictable

    • Law → internalize discipline of social members
