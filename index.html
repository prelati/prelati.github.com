Thoughts on Human Trafficking

2022-02-24

Challenges

Such acts are devoid of statistical representations and therefore more widespread than we thought. They are hard to detect, trace, and be penalized because of their victimization and their ontological meaning. Such crime, like other organizational crimes, presents diffuse, delayed, and less reported victimization. First, its victims cannot be categorized into a specific group or a particular individual. Second, victims will notice their predicament mostly at the end of the whole process (or, sadly speaking, at the end of their lives). Based on the reasons above, victims are less likely to report their victimization to law enforcement agencies or the public. Meanwhile, they would also feel shame to tell their experiences and fear reprisals from stakeholders.

Du Bois -- Veil & Double Consciousness

2022-02-23

He accept the difference by race not only the biology diversity but also the socio-cultural variations, rather than forming a color blindness society with race left off the table. However, such difference is not absolute, meaning not “one of a wall”, but rather a membrane-like “veil” with certain interflows allowed. That is also how our society works: we cannot live solely without any interactions with other people. Thus, in the American society, no matter how racial or housing segregation those white supremacists vociferate for, they would not stab their own eyes to ignore the black community. They have to witness the development of black community and culture through which could form their perspective of the world, so does the black people. American society here has formed a subtle balance between these forces. However, this scale has been inclined by the overwhelmingly dominant white mainstream society in the U.S. history, considering the history of slavery and comparably middle and low socio-economical status of black community. As the double-consciousness theory mentioned, black people have both self-consciousness inherited from their blood and from the white-favored social norm, which potentially provide them a need to express or “reintegrate” to the society.
